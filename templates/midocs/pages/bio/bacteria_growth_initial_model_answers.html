{% intlink bacteria_growth_initial_model thoughtexperiment normaltext %}Thought experiment initial question{%endintlink%}

Question: Suppose the time it takes for a cell to mature and divide is $\tau$ units of time. What fraction of the cells should divide in each time step, where a time step is one unit of time?

Answer: In $\tau$ time steps, every cell should divide once. Therefore, in one time step, the fraction $1/\tau$ of the population should divide. That way, in $\tau$ time steps, the fraction dividing would be $\tau \cdot 1/\tau = 1$. This argument does not require that $\tau$ be an integer, so that if $\tau=5/2$, we'd expect $2/5$ of the population to divide in each time step.

{% navigation_tag "thoughtexperiment2" "Thought experiment answers" "Followup question" %}

{% intlink bacteria_growth_initial_model thoughtexperiment2 normaltext %}Thought experiment followup question{%endintlink%}

Question: Suppose a plot of the change in bacteria density versus the density is well fit by a line through the origin at slope $2/3$. Estimate the time required for a cell to mature and divide.

Answer: From {% intlink bacteria_growth_initial_model_answers thoughtexperiment1 %}above{%endintlink%}, we know that if the cells mature and divide every $\tau$ time steps, then the fraction $1/\tau$ of the population should divide every time step. A slope of $2/3$ means that $2/3$ of the cells divide every time step. Therefore, $2/3=1/\tau$, and the time for a cell to mature and divide is $\tau = 3/2$ time steps.

If each time step is 16 minutes, then the time for a cell to mature and divide is $16 \cdot \frac{3}{2} = 24$ minutes.

{% intlink bacteria_growth_initial_model_exercises exercise5 normaltext %}Exercise 5{%endintlink%}

In a plot of $B_{t+1}-B_t$ versus $B_t$, the first four points fall close to a line. The slope of the line is approximately 0.7. An approximate model is \[ B_{t+1} - B_t = 0.7 B_t \]

The means that approximately 70 percent of the cells divide in each time interval, which is slightly more than we found for a pH of 6.25. A solution to the dynamical system is \[ B_t = B_0 \times 1.7^t = 0.028 \times 1.7^t. \]

The resulting values from the model are close to those of the data except, perhaps, for the last value.
