The degree of a node is the number of edges connected to the node. In terms of the {% intlink "adjacency_matrix_definition" %}adjacency matrix{%endintlink%} $A$, the degree for a node indexed by $i$ in an {% intlink "undirected_graph_definition" %}undirected network{%endintlink%} is \begin{gather*} k_i=\sum_j a_{ij}, \end{gather*} where the sum is over all nodes in the network.

In a {% intlink "directed_graph_definition" %}directed network{%endintlink%}, each node has two degrees. The out-degree is the number of outgoing edges emanating from a node \begin{gather*} k_i^{\text{out}}=\sum_j a_{ji}, \end{gather*} and the in-degree is the number of incoming edges onto a node \begin{gather*} k_i^{\text{in}}=\sum_j a_{ij}. \end{gather*} The total degree of the node is the sum of its in- and out-degree \begin{gather*} k_i^{\text{tot}} = k_i^{\text{in}} + k_i^{\text{out}}. \end{gather*}
