To illustrate our approach for {% intlink distance_point_plane %}finding the distance between a point and a plane{%endintlink%}, we work through an example.

Find the distance from the point $P=(4,-4,3)$ to the plane $2x-2y+5z+8=0$, which is pictured in the below figure in its original view (which you can restore by pressing Home).

{% boxedapplet "distance_point_plane" %}

Solution: We use the {%intlink distance_point_plane distance_formula_2 %}second formula{%endintlink%} from the {%intlink distance_point_plane %}page on distance from a point to a plane{%endintlink%}. From the equation for the plane we substitute $A=2$, $B=-2$, $C=5$, $D=8$. From the point $P$, we substitute $x_1=4$, $y_1=-4$, and $z_1=3$. The distance from $P$ to the plane is \begin{align*} d = \frac{|2 \cdot 4 + (-2)\cdot(-4) + 5 \cdot 3 + 8 |} {\sqrt{2^2+(-2)^2 + 5^2}} = \frac{39}{\sqrt{33}} \approx 6.8 \end{align*}
