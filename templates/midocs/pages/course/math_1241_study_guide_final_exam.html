The final exam for Math 1241 is based on parts 2-6 from the Math 1241 thread. The following highlights what is and what is not good potential material for this exam.

  1. Discrete-time dynamical systems (Discrete dynamical systems from Math 1241 thread).

    See {% intlink math_1241_study_guide_discrete_dynamical_systems %}study guide for Math 1241 discrete dynamical system module exam{%endintlink%}.

  2. Differentiation (The derivative from Math 1241 thread).

    See {% intlink math_1241_study_guide_differentiation %}study guide for Math 1241 differentiation module exam{% endintlink %}

  3. Applications of differentiation (Applications of differentiation from Math 1241 thread).

    See {% intlink math_1241_study_guide_applications_of_differentiation %}study guide for Math 1241 applications of differentiation module exam{% endintlink %}

  4. Autonomous differential equations (Autonomous differential equations from Math 1241 thread).

    See {% intlink math_1241_study_guide_autonomous_differential_equation %}study guide for Math 1241 autonomous differential equation module exam{% endintlink %}

  5. Two-dimensional autonomous differential equations (Two-dimensional autonomous differential equations from Math 1241 thread).

    1. What it means for two autonomous differential equations to be coupled.
    2. Estimating a solution to a two-dimensional differential equation using the Forward Euler algorithm
    3. Understanding the correspondence between the phase plane plot and plots versus time.
    4. Finding equilibria by solving the two equations made by setting the rates of change to zero.
    5. Plotting nullclines in the phase plane.
    6. Finding equilibria by finding the points where the nullclines intersect.
    7. Plotting direction arrows on the nullclines and in the regions of the phase plane that are divided by the nullclines.
    8. Sketching a solution in the phase plane based on direction arrows.
    9. The predator-prey system, the competition equations, the disease model, and the neuron model are good example equations, but you aren't expected to memorize them.
